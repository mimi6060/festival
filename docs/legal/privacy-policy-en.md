## 8. Your Rights

### 8.1 Right of Access (Article 15 GDPR)

You can obtain:
- Confirmation that your data is being processed
- A copy of your personal data
- Information about the processing

### 8.2 Right to Rectification (Article 16 GDPR)

You can have corrected:
- Inaccurate data
- Incomplete data

### 8.3 Right to Erasure (Article 17 GDPR)

You can request deletion of your data if:
- They are no longer necessary
- You withdraw your consent
- You object to the processing
- The processing is unlawful

**Limitations:** This right does not apply if the data is necessary for a legal obligation or for legal proceedings.

### 8.4 Right to Restriction (Article 18 GDPR)

You can request processing be frozen if:
- You contest the accuracy of the data
- The processing is unlawful but you do not want deletion
- We no longer need the data but you require them for legal proceedings
- You have objected to the processing

### 8.5 Right to Data Portability (Article 20 GDPR)

You can receive your data in a structured format (JSON, CSV) and transmit them to another data controller.

### 8.6 Right to Object (Article 21 GDPR)

You can object to:
- Processing based on legitimate interest (for reasons related to your particular situation)
- Processing for direct marketing purposes (without reason)

### 8.7 Right Not to be Subject to Automated Decision-Making (Article 22 GDPR)

No decision producing legal effects concerning you is made solely on the basis of automated processing.

### 8.8 Post-Mortem Directives

You can define directives regarding the fate of your data after your death.
