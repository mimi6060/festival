## 1. Definitions

For the purposes of this Agreement, the following terms shall have the meanings set out below. Terms not defined herein shall have the meanings ascribed to them in the GDPR.

### 1.1 Core GDPR Definitions

**"Personal Data"** means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person ("Data Subject"); an identifiable natural person is one who can be identified, directly or indirectly, in particular by reference to an identifier such as a name, an identification number, location data, an online identifier or to one or more factors specific to the physical, physiological, genetic, mental, economic, cultural or social identity of that natural person.

**"Data Controller"** or **"Controller"** means the natural or legal person, public authority, agency or other body which, alone or jointly with others, determines the purposes and means of the processing of Personal Data. For the purposes of this Agreement, the Controller is the entity identified in the Preamble.

**"Data Processor"** or **"Processor"** means a natural or legal person, public authority, agency or other body which processes Personal Data on behalf of the Controller. For the purposes of this Agreement, the Processor is Festival Tech SAS.

**"Data Subject"** means an identified or identifiable natural person whose Personal Data is processed under this Agreement. In the context of the Festival Management Platform, Data Subjects include but are not limited to:
- Festival attendees and ticket purchasers
- Cashless account holders
- Festival staff and volunteers
- Vendor and partner representatives
- Users of the platform's web and mobile applications

**"Processing"** means any operation or set of operations which is performed on Personal Data or on sets of Personal Data, whether or not by automated means, such as collection, recording, organisation, structuring, storage, adaptation or alteration, retrieval, consultation, use, disclosure by transmission, dissemination or otherwise making available, alignment or combination, restriction, erasure or destruction.

**"Sub-processor"** means any Processor engaged by the Processor to process Personal Data on behalf of the Controller under this Agreement.

### 1.2 Additional Definitions

**"Applicable Data Protection Law"** means all laws and regulations applicable to the processing of Personal Data under this Agreement, including but not limited to:
- The General Data Protection Regulation (EU) 2016/679 (GDPR)
- The UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR)
- The French Data Protection Act (Loi Informatique et Libertes)
- Any other applicable national data protection legislation

**"Data Breach"** or **"Personal Data Breach"** means a breach of security leading to the accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorised disclosure of, or access to, Personal Data transmitted, stored or otherwise processed.

**"DPA"** means this Data Processing Agreement.

**"EEA"** means the European Economic Area.

**"Festival Platform"** or **"Platform"** means the Festival Management Platform software and services provided by the Processor, including all web applications, mobile applications, APIs, and related infrastructure.

**"Security Measures"** means the technical and organisational measures implemented by the Processor to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk of processing Personal Data.

**"Special Categories of Personal Data"** means Personal Data revealing racial or ethnic origin, political opinions, religious or philosophical beliefs, trade union membership, genetic data, biometric data for the purpose of uniquely identifying a natural person, data concerning health, or data concerning a natural person's sex life or sexual orientation.

**"Standard Contractual Clauses"** or **"SCCs"** means the contractual clauses adopted by the European Commission for the transfer of Personal Data to third countries pursuant to Article 46(2)(c) GDPR.

**"Supervisory Authority"** means an independent public authority established by a Member State pursuant to Article 51 GDPR.

**"Third Country"** means a country outside the European Economic Area that has not been recognised by the European Commission as providing an adequate level of data protection.

## 8. Personal Data Breach Notification

### 8.1 The 72-Hour Requirement

In accordance with Article 33 GDPR, the Controller must notify the competent Supervisory Authority of a Personal Data Breach within 72 hours of becoming aware of it, unless the breach is unlikely to result in a risk to the rights and freedoms of natural persons.

### 8.2 Processor Notification Obligation

The Processor shall notify the Controller without undue delay after becoming aware of a Personal Data Breach. The notification timeline is as follows:

| Breach Severity | Initial Notification | Full Report |
|-----------------|---------------------|-------------|
| **Critical** (active data exfiltration, ongoing attack) | Within 4 hours | Within 24 hours |
| **High** (confirmed breach with data exposure) | Within 8 hours | Within 48 hours |
| **Medium** (potential breach, investigation ongoing) | Within 24 hours | Within 72 hours |
| **Low** (contained incident, minimal exposure) | Within 48 hours | Within 5 business days |

### 8.3 Content of Breach Notification

The Processor's notification to the Controller shall include, to the extent known:

**(a) Initial Notification:**
- Description of the nature of the breach
- Approximate date and time of the breach
- Categories of data and Data Subjects affected (if known)
- Name and contact details of the DPO or other contact point
- Immediate actions taken to contain the breach

**(b) Full Report:**
- Detailed description of the breach and its circumstances
- Categories and approximate number of Data Subjects concerned
- Categories and approximate number of Personal Data records concerned
- Likely consequences of the breach
- Measures taken or proposed to address the breach
- Measures taken or proposed to mitigate possible adverse effects
- Root cause analysis (when available)
- Timeline of events

### 8.4 Assistance to Controller

The Processor shall assist the Controller in:

**(a)** Fulfilling the Controller's obligation to notify the Supervisory Authority

**(b)** Fulfilling the Controller's obligation to communicate the breach to Data Subjects where required by Article 34 GDPR

**(c)** Investigating and documenting the breach

**(d)** Implementing remedial measures to address the breach and prevent recurrence

### 8.5 Breach Response Procedures

The Processor maintains documented breach response procedures including:

1. **Detection & Identification**
   - Automated monitoring and alerting
   - Employee reporting channels
   - Third-party notification handling

2. **Containment & Eradication**
   - Immediate containment measures
   - Evidence preservation
   - Threat elimination

3. **Assessment & Analysis**
   - Impact assessment
   - Risk evaluation
   - Root cause analysis

4. **Notification & Communication**
   - Controller notification
   - Regulatory notification support
   - Data Subject communication support

5. **Remediation & Recovery**
   - System restoration
   - Control improvements
   - Process updates

6. **Documentation & Review**
   - Incident documentation
   - Lessons learned
   - Policy/procedure updates

### 8.6 Breach Register

The Processor shall maintain a register of all Personal Data Breaches, including:
- Facts relating to the breach
- Effects of the breach
- Remedial actions taken

This register shall be made available to the Controller upon request.

### 8.7 Communication Coordination

The Parties shall coordinate all public communications regarding any Personal Data Breach. The Controller shall approve any public statement before release, unless immediate disclosure is required by law.

## 9. Audit Rights

### 9.1 Controller Audit Rights

The Controller has the right to conduct audits to verify the Processor's compliance with this Agreement and Applicable Data Protection Law.

### 9.2 Audit Scope

Audits may cover:

| Area | Examples |
|------|----------|
| **Technical Security** | Infrastructure security, encryption, access controls, vulnerability management |
| **Organisational Measures** | Policies, procedures, training, personnel security |
| **Data Processing Activities** | Processing records, data flows, retention practices |
| **Sub-processor Management** | Sub-processor agreements, oversight, due diligence |
| **Incident Management** | Breach procedures, incident records, response capabilities |
| **Compliance** | GDPR compliance, certification status, regulatory correspondence |

### 9.3 Audit Procedures

**(a) Notice**
The Controller shall provide at least 30 days' written notice of an intended audit, except in the case of:
- Regulatory investigation or request
- Suspected Personal Data Breach
- Material non-compliance concerns

In such cases, reasonable notice shall be provided.

**(b) Frequency**
- Regular audits: Maximum one per 12-month period
- Additional audits: As reasonably required following a Personal Data Breach or material non-compliance

**(c) Conduct**
Audits shall:
- Be conducted during normal business hours
- Not unreasonably interfere with the Processor's operations
- Comply with the Processor's security and confidentiality requirements
- Be conducted by qualified auditors bound by confidentiality obligations

**(d) Third-Party Auditors**
The Controller may engage independent third-party auditors, subject to:
- Prior written approval by the Processor (not to be unreasonably withheld)
- Auditor's execution of appropriate confidentiality agreements
- Auditor's compliance with Processor security requirements

### 9.4 Audit Alternatives

In lieu of Controller audits, the Processor may provide:

**(a) Certifications and Reports**
- ISO 27001 certification and audit reports
- SOC 2 Type II reports
- PCI-DSS attestation of compliance
- Third-party penetration test reports (summary)

**(b) Compliance Questionnaires**
- Completed industry-standard security questionnaires (e.g., SIG, CAIQ)
- Responses to Controller-specific questionnaires

**(c) Documentation**
- Relevant policies and procedures
- Processing records
- Security documentation

### 9.5 Audit Findings

**(a) Remediation**
If an audit identifies non-compliance, the Processor shall:
1. Acknowledge the finding in writing within 5 business days
2. Provide a remediation plan within 15 business days
3. Implement remediation according to the agreed timeline
4. Provide evidence of remediation upon completion

**(b) Material Non-Compliance**
If material non-compliance is identified that poses a significant risk to Personal Data, the Processor shall:
1. Implement immediate interim measures
2. Provide daily progress updates until resolved
3. Accept additional audit rights until compliance is demonstrated

### 9.6 Audit Costs

- The Controller bears the costs of audits initiated by the Controller
- The Processor bears the costs of remediation of identified non-compliance
- If an audit reveals material non-compliance, the Processor shall reimburse the Controller's reasonable audit costs
